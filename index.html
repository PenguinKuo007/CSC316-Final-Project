How do smokers vs. non-smokers compare at each lung cancer stage? What is their mortality risk at each stage?

The visualization shows that most smokers with lung cancer are at stage III and stage IV, while most non-smokers with lung cancer are at stage I and stage II. The visualization also shows that mortality risk increases as lung cancer stage increases, with smokers having a higher mortality risk than non-smokers at each stage. These insights suggest that smoking may be associated with not only being diagnosed at more advanced lung cancer stages, but also being more at risk of mortality at each stage than non-smokers.

Overall, the main message to take away from these visualizations is:

Smokers should quit smoking to lower their risk of developing lung cancer and to prevent their condition from worsening.

Resources to Help Smokers Quit Smoking

  • In Canada, smokers can talk to a quit coach for advice by calling the Smokers' Helpline at 1-866-366-3667.
  • Health Canada provides an infographic with "Tips for Staying Smoke-free".
  • Health Canada also provides an in-depth "Guide to becoming a non-smoker".
  • The Canadian Cancer Society has a Quit Map that smokers can use to search for services online or in-person.
